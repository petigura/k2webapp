DV summary plots.

Top row: SNR “periodogram” of box-car photometric search for transiting planets. The red dot shows the P and SNR of the most significant peak in the periodogram. The autocorrelation function, “ACF”, plot at upper right shows the circular auto-correlation function from the phase folded photometry, used to identify secondary eclipses and correlated noise in the photometry. Second row: at left “Phase” shows the phase–folded photometry, where black points represent detrended photometry near the time of transit, green symbols show median flux over 30 min bins, and the dashed line shows the best-fitting Mandel-Agol transit model (7). “Phased Zoom” shows a zoomed y-axis to highlight the transit itself. “Phased 180” shows phase folded photometry 180 deg in orbital phase from transit center. At fourth from left, “Secondary eclipse” shows how TERRA notches out the putative transit and searches for secondary eclipses. Transit SES — transit single event statistic as a function of transit number. Bottom row: At left, “SES stack” shows SES for the entire light curve, split on the best-fitting transit period and stacked so that transit number increases downward. Compelling transits appear as a sequence of SES peaks at phase = 0.
